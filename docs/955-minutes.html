City of Somerville, Massachusetts City Council Confirmation of Appointments and Personnel Matters Committee Meeting Minutes 6:00 PM Tuesday, September 13, 2022 Roll Call This meeting was held via GoToWebinar and was called to order by Chair Pineda Neufeld at 6:02 pm and adjourned at 7:19 pm. Councilor Wilson moved to adjourn, and the motion was approved by roll call vote. Others present: Hannah Carrillo - Mayor’s Office, Cathy Piantigini - Libraries, Janaki Perera, Timothy Zessin - KP Law, Jason Piques - Law Office, Mark Sternman, Ashley Speliotis - Council on Aging, Neeraj Wadhera, Stephanie Widzowski - Assistant Clerk of Committees. Jake Wilson, City Councilor At Large Kristen Strezo and Ward Seven City Councilor Judy Pineda Neufeld Present: Jesse Clingan and Charlotte Kelly Absent: 1. By Councilor Pineda Neufeld That the City Solicitor update this Council on the 8/11/22 Civil Service Commission decision Fairchild, Matthew v. City of Somerville, and the impact on the confirmations process. Order (ID # 22-1459) Chair Pineda Neufeld asked Mr. Zessin how he would recommend the committee shift its appointment process after this decision. Mr. Zessin explained that the Civil Service Commission was trying to balance Matthew Fairchild’s rights with the Council’s right to review appointments. They did not state that the Confirmation of Appointments committee could not conduct a review, but there needs to be good reason when bypassing a candidate. He said that their ruling affects the scope and direction of review. He also talked about how the anti-discrimination statute and the Americans with Disabilities Act impose more restrictions on the city as a whole than Chapter 31, which is the statute referred to by the commission. Councilor Strezo asked how much this case will cost the city, and about the viability of executive sessions. Mr. Zessin said that he could get the cost information for Councilor Strezo at another time. He also replied that the appellant would have certain rights to the discussion that led to the bypass appeal. Councilor Wilson asked if the political considerations from the ruling refer to the stance of not approving police appointments in the wake of George Floyd’s death. By Councilor Pineda Neufeld That the Committee on Confirmation of Appointments and Personnel Matters discuss and revise the process for reviewing appointments and re-appointments. Order (ID # 22-0209) Councilor Wilson asked if the sequencing of the appointment process should be changed. If so, it would need to be a full City Council discussion. Councilor Strezo commented that this topic had been addressed before, and that the councilors on the Confirmation of Appointments committee make the decisions to bring to the full council. Chair Pineda Neufeld said that she preferred to make a decision on this item when all committee members are present. KEPT IN COMMITTEE RESULT: 3. Requesting confirmation of the appointment of Neeraj Wadhera to the Council on Aging. Mayor's Request (ID # 22-1087) Dir. Speliotis spoke to Ms. Wadhera’s history in Somerville and long-time experience at Fidelity. Councilor Wilson asked the candidate what Somerville could do better for seniors. Ms. Wadhera said that her observations are limited to her visits to the Senior Center, but that there should be a choice of activities that would engage a diverse group of seniors.
